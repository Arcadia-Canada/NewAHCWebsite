## Audience

The reader for this article is slightly different from the emotional Master Prompt pieces:

- Adult child or spouse researching options
- Living in Toronto or the GTA
- In the early-to-mid decision stage (not yet committed, comparing options)
- Searching with intent: "how much does home care cost," "PSW cost Ontario," "home care pricing Toronto"
- Likely comparing home care to retirement homes, long-term care, or doing nothing

This person needs numbers, not feelings. They are trying to determine whether home care is financially viable for their family before they invest time in consultations and assessments.

The share target: A sibling who assumes home care is unaffordable. A spouse doing budget research. A friend whose parent just came home from hospital.

## Tone

Practical. Direct. Transparent. This is not a sales piece and must never read like one.

The biggest risk is sounding like a brochure or a pricing page. This is an educational article that happens to include numbers. The voice should be "knowledgeable person explaining clearly" not "company pitching its rates."

**Avoid:**
- "Affordable home care" or "budget-friendly" language
- Urgency or scarcity framing
- Exclamation points or enthusiasm
- "We offer competitive rates" or comparison to specific competitors
- Minimizing the cost ("it's only $X per hour!")
- Guaranteeing specific rates (rates vary by situation)

**Lean into:**
- Specificity: actual GTA ranges, not vague "it depends"
- Comparison to alternatives (retirement homes, long-term care)
- The public + private combination that most families use
- Starting small: you do not need 40 hours/week to benefit
- Tax credits and insurance as real cost-reduction mechanisms
- The disclaimer: consult a tax professional for specific advice
