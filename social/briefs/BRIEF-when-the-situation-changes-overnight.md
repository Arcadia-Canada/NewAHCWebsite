## What this article is

Master Prompt article for families hit by a sudden change: a fall, a hospital discharge, a fast decline, or a primary caregiver who has run out of capacity. It sits in Navigating Home Care as the crisis-entry counterpart to "Signs Your Parent Needs Home Care" (the slow-pattern piece). Where that article helps families see a gradual shift, this one meets the family on the day everything speeds up. Links directly to hospital discharge support and post-hospital recovery.

The emotional entry point: Yesterday your parent was managing. Then there was a fall, or a phone call from the hospital, or a morning when something was suddenly wrong.

The key reframe: In a crisis, decide for the next 48 hours, not the next six months. Most sudden situations can be stabilized at home long enough to make the larger decisions calmly.

## Audience

- Adult daughter or son in their 40s, 50s, 60s
- Toronto / GTA
- In or just past an acute event: a fall, an ER visit, a hospital discharge, a sudden decline
- Under time pressure and frightened, being pushed toward permanent decisions on an emergency timeline

Share target: a sibling arriving from out of town who wants to "sort out the whole plan this weekend," or a friend whose parent was just admitted.

## Tone

Steady and grounding, not alarmist. Reduce panic by narrowing the decision. Give people permission to solve the next 48 hours instead of the whole future.

**Avoid:** urgency-as-sales, catastrophizing, exclamation points, corporate voice, implying a facility is the default answer.

**Lean into:** the 48-hour frame, what can wait, arranging care before discharge, protecting the caregiver.
