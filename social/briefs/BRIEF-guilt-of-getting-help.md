## What this article is

Master Prompt article for family caregivers who know they need help and feel like asking for it means they have failed. It sits in Family Caregiver Support beside burnout and weight-of-caregiving: the piece that names the moral barrier that keeps families stuck after they already understand the load.

The emotional entry point: You know something needs to change. And still, when someone mentions home care, something in you tightens: if you were a better daughter or son, you would not need this.

The key reframe: Getting help is not abandoning your parent. It rearranges labour. You are not being replaced. You are being relieved of work that was never meant to be done alone.

## Audience

- Adult daughter or son in their 40s, 50s, 60s
- Toronto / GTA
- Already carrying substantial care
- Stuck between knowing help is needed and feeling disloyal for wanting it

Share target: a sibling who moralizes "family should do it," or a spouse who sees the guilt more clearly than the caregiver does.

## Tone

Permission and clarity, not pep talk. Validate the guilt without treating it as a reason to continue alone.

**Avoid:** motivational slogans, abandonment language flipped into shame, salesy urgency, exclamation points, corporate voice.

**Lean into:** loyalty vs labour, smaller starts, identity ("the reliable one"), concrete first steps.
