## What this article is

A foundational role-explainer for the Navigating Home Care cluster. PSWs (Personal Support Workers) are the backbone of home care in Ontario, but most families do not have a clear picture of what the role covers, what it does not, or what makes a good match.

This article answers the question families ask most often after "how much does it cost?" It pairs naturally with the cost article published the same day.

The article owns the keyword "what does a PSW do" and supports related searches around PSW training, scope of practice, and the PSW vs. nurse distinction.

The emotional entry point: Most families know the term PSW. Far fewer feel confident about what to expect from one. This article fills that gap honestly.

The key reframe: A PSW is more than a service line. For most families, the PSW becomes a person whose consistent presence transforms the daily experience of caring for a parent. Understanding the role and its limits helps families set up arrangements that actually work.

## Tone

Educational. Direct. Respectful of the role. PSWs do hard, important work and the article must reflect that without sentimentalizing.

The biggest risks: making PSWs sound like a commodity (purely transactional), or sounding patronizing (talking down to families). Neither lands well.

**Avoid:**
- Listing PSW duties without context
- Comparing PSWs unfavourably to nurses
- "Our PSWs are the best" promotional framing
- Treating the role as simple or low-skill
- Dismissive language about training or scope

**Lean into:**
- Specifics: actual examples of what a PSW does in a visit
- Honesty about the PSW vs. nurse distinction
- The observational role: PSWs notice changes families miss
- Continuity as the key success factor
- The relationship dimension: PSWs become trusted presences in the home
