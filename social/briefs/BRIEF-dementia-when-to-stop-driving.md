## What this article is

The driving question is one of the earliest "role reversal" moments for families navigating dementia. This article catches the reader at the point where they have noticed unsafe behaviour behind the wheel but have not yet acted, either because they are unsure whether what they are seeing is serious enough, or because they dread the conversation.

It sits inside the Dementia & Memory Care cluster as a practical guide that bridges the gap between "noticing early signs" and "arranging support." Unlike the early-signs article (which validates observation), this one demands action, and gives the reader a path to take it.

The emotional entry point: You have noticed the hesitation at intersections. The new scrape on the bumper with no explanation. The near-miss in the parking lot that your parent brushed off but you cannot stop replaying.

The key reframe: Good days do not cancel out dangerous ones. The inconsistency of safe and unsafe driving is itself the pattern, not a reason to wait longer. And removing the keys is not cruelty; it is the same kind of quiet protection as locking the stove or camouflaging the front door.

## Tone

This is a "permission to act" article. The reader already knows the answer but needs external validation to do the hard thing. The tone must be calm, direct, and honest about the difficulty without either minimizing it or dramatizing it.

The biggest voice risk is drifting toward clinical safety checklists or patronizing "have the talk" framing. The value is in naming the emotional weight of the decision (independence, adulthood, control) while providing a clear, practical path forward.

**Avoid:**
- Listicle framing ("7 signs it's time to take the keys")
- Clinical language ("cognitive impairment affecting psychomotor function")
- Alarmist urgency ("Every day you wait is a day someone could die")
- "You've got this!" motivational framing
- Exclamation points
- "We at Arcadia believe..." or any corporate pivot

**Lean into:**
- The specific driving moments the reader recognizes (intersection hesitation, unexplained scrapes)
- The emotional truth: this feels like taking away adulthood
- Permission language: "You do not have to wait for a crash"
- Practical Ontario-specific paths (physician reporting, DriveABLE, MTO process)
- The replacement frame: plan transportation before removing driving
